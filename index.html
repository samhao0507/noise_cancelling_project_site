Introduction

This website is created for presenting the outcomes of the final project of EECS 351: Intro to Digital Signal Processing class at University of Michigan by Group 11 - Yin Siang Hao, Ronald Kreucher, Harry Lyu, Yi Rong.

Inspired by multiple musical instruments and devices we hold at home, our project focuses on removing the ambient audio noise in audio recordings. We focus on cancelling the ambient noise, such as dog barking, raining, and sea waves, which are prevalent in musical recordings. We first tried out simple filtering on the recordings to see the effect. Later, we used multiple DSP techniques, including Analysis in a Different Basis, Frequency Domain Analysis (STFT), Sampling (Resampling). We used a CNN model to better restore the clean audio and this implements Image processing techniques. Our final task is to produce a system that can cancel out ambient noise and restore the cleanest recordings. And we used recordings made by ourselves to test the models and show the results.
