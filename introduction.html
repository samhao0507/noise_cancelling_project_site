Introduction

Instrumental recordings often pick up background sounds that are hard to remove without damaging the music. In this project we focus on a concrete case: separating people chattering from instrumental tracks. Our goal is to recover a cleaner version of the instrumental and to compare a traditional filtering approach to a learning-based model so we can see which method performs better on denoising.

During the process, we used multiple DSP techniques, including various filtering methods (bandpass and notch filtering), analysis on a different basis, STFT (frequency domain analysis), and resampling (sampling). On the Methods page we document two paths. First, a multi-stage basic filtering baseline (DC removal, band-pass and optional notch filtering, wavelet denoising, and soft frame-wise attenuation) that stays interpretable and gives a reference for comparison. Second, a U-Net trained on STFT magnitude inputs: the network predicts an ideal ratio mask between clean and noisy magnitudes so it can suppress noise-dominated time-frequency bins. The Data section summarizes sources and preprocessing; Results shows listening examples and spectrum-level comparisons for both the filter and the U-Net.
